<b>Definition:</b> This<b> </b>is a representation of an item that will be physically handled, loaned or moved in collection moves.<br/><b>Scope:</b> A <b>CollectionObject </b>may link to one or more <b>IdentifiableItem</b>s that equate to one or more taxa that are present in the <b>CollectionObject</b>. As well as the specimen(s) or sample(s), the <b>CollectionObject</b> also includes any physical media used to preserve and present them, such as a microscope slide, herbarium sheet or jar.<br/><b>Context: </b>A <b>CollectionObject</b> can have further <b>CollectionObject</b>s derived from it<br/><b>Examples:</b> <br/><ul>
<li>This simplest case is one <b>CollectionObject</b> and one <b>IdentifiableItem</b> which describe different attributes of the are the same physical object (for example, a single insect on a pin).</li><li>When we have a parasite on the insect, we have two different species and so two <b>IdentifiableItem</b>s that are not separate collection objects.</li><li>One large <b>IdentifiableItem</b>, e.g. a large skeleton can have components stored in multiple locations simultaneously, so will be represented by multiple related <b>CollectionObject</b>s.</li></ul>
