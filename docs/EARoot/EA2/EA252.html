<b>Definition</b>: This describes the event of how different <b>CollectionObject</b>s came to be related to each other<br/><b>Scope: </b>New child <b>CollectionObject</b>s are created in situations such as:<br/><ul>
<li>a specimen is split for analysis.</li><li>an item on display is taken apart for storage.</li><li>A new organism is bred or grown</li></ul>
A new parent <b>CollectionObject</b> are created when items are combined from different <b>StorageLocation</b>s into a single item. e.g. a bound volume of herbarium sheet is made.<br/><b>Context</b>: There may also be other relationships between <b>CollectionObject</b>s , e.g. synecology, the relationship between different taxa collected at the <b>Locality</b> (prey, feeding etc)<br/><b>External References</b>:<br/><a href="file://https://dwc.tdwg.org/list/"><font color="#0066cc"><u>dwc:associatedSequences</u></font></a><br/><br/><i><u>EMU Types:</u></i><br/><i>AssBilogicalRelationship_tab - these are one way, and not reciprocated in EMu)</i><br/><ul>
